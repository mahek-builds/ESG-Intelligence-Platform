ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) Framework:

ENVIRONMENTAL:
- Carbon emissions reduction and climate change impact
- Renewable energy usage and energy efficiency
- Waste management and circular economy practices
- Water resource management
- Biodiversity and ecosystem preservation

SOCIAL:
- Labor practices and employee welfare
- Community engagement and stakeholder relations
- Supply chain responsibility
- Health and safety standards
- Diversity and inclusion initiatives

GOVERNANCE:
- Board structure and composition
- Executive compensation and incentives
- Shareholder rights and voting policies
- Ethics and anti-corruption programs
- Risk management and compliance
- Transparency and disclosure standards

ESG Scoring Methodology:
- Environmental Score: 0-100 based on carbon efficiency and green initiatives
- Social Score: 0-100 based on labor practices and community impact
- Governance Score: 0-100 based on board quality and transparency
- Weighted ESG Score: Combined metric for overall performance
- Risk Category: Low (score >70), Medium (50-70), High (<50)

ESG Benefits:
- Improved financial performance and investor returns
- Risk mitigation and regulatory compliance
- Enhanced brand reputation and customer loyalty
- Employee satisfaction and retention
- Access to sustainable financing and ESG-focused funds
